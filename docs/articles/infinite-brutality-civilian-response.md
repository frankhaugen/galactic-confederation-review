> Author: Professor Eleanor Hayes, Department of Ethics, University of Earth

## Editorial note

This article was selected after extensive correspondence concerning Earth Fleet boarding doctrine and its treatment in veteran historical literature.

The Review notes that the Earth Fleet Historical Society response has not been selected for republication at this time.

## Abstract

Earth Fleet boarding doctrine describes itself as rescue-oriented violence. This article argues that a doctrine may be tactically humane while remaining morally impermissible if it treats the deliberate killing of hostages or hostage-adjacent persons as an acceptable rescue cost.
