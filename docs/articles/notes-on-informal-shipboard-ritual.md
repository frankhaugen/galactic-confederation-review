## Editorial note

Selected as an accessible example of frontier anthropological observation. The Review notes that Dr. Whitaker's original submission included three appendices on galley seating behavior. These are omitted from this republication.

## Abstract

Independent merchant crews develop informal rituals that compensate for weak institutional structure, low crew count, mixed-species domesticity, and long-duration transit. This paper argues that such rituals function as procedural glue rather than superstition.
