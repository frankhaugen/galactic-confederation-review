# Editorial Policy

The Galactic Confederation Review selects works according to public-interest relevance, cross-polity usefulness, argumentative quality, and long-term archival value.

## Selection criteria

A work may be republished when it:

- clarifies an institution, practice, or dispute of wide relevance
- presents a significant technical, legal, ethical, historical, or economic argument
- has influenced policy or public debate
- represents a major controversy
- provides unusually clear summary of a complex subject
- has become widely cited outside its original discipline

## Non-endorsement

Republication does not imply agreement.

The Review publishes incompatible arguments when each is useful to wider discourse.

## Editorial notes

Editors may attach notes to explain:

- original publication context
- subsequent controversy
- archival limitations
- translation issues
- later developments
- related selections

## Corrections

The Review preserves the original argument where possible. Corrections address transcription, translation, metadata, and republication errors.

Substantive rebuttals are normally published as separate works.
