!!! editorial "Editorial introduction"
    The Review reserves at least one issue per register year for **hard science** — work where the mechanism matters. This cycle: FTL tradeoffs for policymakers, the founding container standard and its social afterlife, registered hull personality and the commercial-law cluster that made freedom of navigation operable, passenger liability for small operators, an owner-master memoir, and the Confederation's two-tier framework for artificial cognition paired with Dr. Vosk's taboo essay from the same planning cycle.

    The container pair, the ship-registry essays, the owner-master settlement, and the cognition pair do not share bibliographies. The board judged them share an editorial obligation: explain what the Confederation actually built before arguing about what it means.
