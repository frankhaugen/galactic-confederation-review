# Review Issues

> Galactic Confederation Review · Public archive edition

The Review publishes in **issues**. The editorial board plans each issue around a subject readers can name in one sentence: *this one is Guardianship*, *that one is Communications*, *spring hard science*.

Individual articles retain their own **selection dates** — the cycle day each piece entered the register. The board may hold selections across many cycles while assembling a planned issue.

## How issues are planned

The board maintains a running slate: planned subjects, held selections, and missing counterparts. An issue closes when the editorial argument is complete — not when the calendar says so.

Issues are not anthologies of agreement. Incompatible arguments may share an issue when the subject requires it. Paired essays — reply sets, companion pieces, physics-and-policy counterparts — are usually held in the register before the issue closes.

## Calendar note

Confederation standard ordinal notation records year and cycle day: `2496.221` denotes the 221st day of cycle year 2496. **Issue numbers** identify the collection; **release dates** record when the board closed it; **selection dates** mark individual register entries.
