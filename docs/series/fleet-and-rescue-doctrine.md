# Fleet and Rescue Doctrine

The Fleet and Rescue Doctrine series republishes works on Earth Fleet, Nosies, rescue practice, anti-piracy posture, military ethics, boarding, and the legitimacy claims attached to public force at interstellar scale.

This series is for readers examining how rescue institutions justify violence, restraint, and administrative refusal—not for readers seeking order-of-battle reference or weapons specifications.

## What this series does not cover

Fleet and Rescue Doctrine does not publish comparative criminal law in low-sovereignty corridors, ansible policy, or container standards. Prize recovery and vigilantism lines appear under Comparative Law. Earth Union welfare militarism appears in Earth Union Studies.
